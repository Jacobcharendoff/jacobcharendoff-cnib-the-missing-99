The Gap

The support exists.
Most people never find it.

Sight loss arrives fast and without a roadmap. A diagnosis. A doctor's appointment. A moment in the kitchen when something doesn't look right. And then — nothing. No one tells you what comes next.

CNIB has been building programs, services, and community for Canadians with sight loss for over a hundred years. Free programs. Real support. People who understand. And yet, of the more than 1.5 million Canadians living with sight loss, fewer than 13,000 are currently engaged with what CNIB offers.

That's not a funding problem. It's not a program quality problem. It's a connection problem. People who need support don't know it exists. And when they do find it, they don't know where to start.

iris. is built to solve that. Not by adding more programs. By making sure the right person finds the right one — at the right moment in their life.

The Experience

A guide who actually knows you.

Not a directory. Not a chatbot. A guide that gets to know each member the way a good mentor would — through conversation, over time, across the real moments of their life.

Listens first.
iris. opens with the human question, not the intake form. What brought you here? What's changed? What's been the hardest part? iris. builds a picture of the whole person before suggesting anything.
Matches precisely.
Based on diagnosis, geography, life stage, and what matters most right now, iris. surfaces two or three programs that are actually right — not a list of everything CNIB offers.
Takes the next step.
iris. doesn't end with information. iris. ends with action. A referral booked. A connection made. A follow-up scheduled. The member leaves having gained something real.
Stays.
iris. checks in. As life changes — diagnosis progresses, circumstances shift, new needs emerge — iris. adjusts. The relationship deepens over time. From newly diagnosed to engaged community member.

Not a one-time interaction. A lifelong guide.

The Pilot

Three months. Two approaches.
One question.

What is the most effective way to reach Canadians with sight loss and connect them to what CNIB has built for them?

This is not a product launch. It's a structured learning exercise. We pilot two distinct approaches in parallel, with real members, across three months. We measure what works. We learn who responds to what. And at the end, we have the evidence and confidence to build what we know works — not what we think might.

Approach 1 — Direct member engagement.
iris. delivered through voice and conversation — phone, smart device, or voice assistant. Accessible to members regardless of technology comfort or visual capability. Designed to reach the full demographic spectrum: seniors, newly diagnosed adults, families, long-term members who don't know everything available to them.
Approach 2 — Channel partner distribution.
iris. delivered as a simple referral tool for healthcare and professional partners. An ophthalmologist finishes a difficult conversation and hands a card. A social worker makes a call. A pharmacist passes along a number. iris. picks up from there. Testing whether warm professional referrals drive stronger member engagement than direct outreach.
Month 1
Strategy and build.
Define the target member for each approach. Map the specific problems each prototype solves. Build both prototypes. Identify and onboard channel partners. Establish how we'll measure success.
Month 2
Live testing.
Both approaches live with real members. iris. has real conversations. Partners begin referrals. Weekly learning reviews. Rapid iteration based on what members actually say and do.
Month 3
Learning and confidence.
Synthesize what worked and what didn't across both channels. Understand which member types respond best to which approach. Build the evidence base for what comes next. Present findings and recommendation.
What success looks like.

We're measuring three things. Members who enroll in at least one CNIB program they didn't know existed before talking to iris. Members who return for a second conversation — the signal that iris. earned their trust. Channel partners who refer more than one patient — the signal that the tool is genuinely useful, not just novel.

The pilot is not the product. It's the evidence that makes the product worth building properly. Three months of real conversations with real Canadians tells us more than any amount of planning — and gives CNIB the confidence to invest in what we know works.
