Under the hood

What you just heard
wasn't a script.

Every Iris conversation moves through four layers, in the same order, every time. Story before solutions. Life before recommendations. The immediate need before anything practical. The bigger hope last — because that's the one Iris is actually navigating toward.

The conversation

Story → Life → Need → Hope.

  1. 01
    The Story
    "How did you get here?"
    When was the diagnosis. What's changed. What's been the hardest part to put into words. Iris listens for the emotional truth beneath the facts before anything else.
  2. 02
    The Life
    "Who are you beyond the diagnosis?"
    Work, family, what you used to love, what you're afraid of losing. This is where Iris builds a picture of a whole person — not a condition.
  3. 03
    The Need
    "What's hardest right now? Today. This week."
    Not in general — specifically. Practical, social, professional, financial, or emotional. The immediate need is where Iris starts. That's what earns trust.
  4. 04
    The Hope
    "What does a good life look like, on your terms?"
    Not CNIB's terms. Yours. The north star Iris navigates toward — for as long as the relationship lasts.

Iris doesn't end with information. Iris ends with a name, a number, and a time.

A Vision Mate matched. A program enrolled. A follow-up booked. The member leaves having gained something real — not a brochure.

Your way.
The match

Five inputs. The one program that's right for you, today.

CNIB has a hundred programs. Most don't fit most people. Iris's job isn't to list them — it's to surface the two or three that fit this person, in this place, at this moment in their life.

Diagnosis
Low vision. Total blindness. Deafblind. Newly diagnosed. Degenerative. Stable.
Geography
Province, city, rural or urban. Many programs are region-locked. Iris only surfaces what's actually available to you.
Life stage
Child. Youth. Working age. Senior. New parent. Newly retired. Each stage opens different doors.
Primary concern
Independence. Employment. Connection. Technology. Education. Family. Money. The thing that's loudest right now.
Interests & lifestyle
Active. Creative. Professional. Caregiver. The texture of a real life — so the match feels like a fit, not a referral.

Two ways in

Reaching members directly.
And through every door they already walk through.

There are two reasons most Canadians with sight loss never find CNIB. They don't know it exists. Or they do, and the moment they could have used it passed without anyone handing them a card. Iris was built for both.

01 · Direct

For members
and families.

Iris meets people where they already are — on the phone, on the web, on a smart speaker, by text. Someone newly diagnosed, terrified at 2am. A parent fighting the school system alone. A senior who has slowly stopped leaving the house. A working adult quietly burning out from compensating. Iris picks up. Asks the right questions. Surfaces the right support. And stays.

The Member Journey

Every person. A different path.
One intelligent system.

Iris doesn't follow a script. It maps a unique journey based on diagnosis, life stage, emotional readiness, and goals. Here's what that looks like.

New Diagnosis Journey
1
First Contact — Iris listens. No intake forms. Understands diagnosis, fears, life context.
2
Emotional Support — Peer counseling referral. Vision Mate match. "You're not alone."
3
Practical Tools — Technology assessment. Lending library. Phone accessibility setup.
4
Life Navigation — Employment rights. Driving assessment. Workplace accommodations.
5
Long-term Connection — Community. Sports. Advocacy. From member to champion.
Senior / Phone Journey
1
Warm Outreach — Iris calls. Warm, unhurried. No pressure. Builds trust.
2
Safety Assessment — Falls, kitchen, reading, isolation. Identifies urgent needs.
3
Home Visit — Rehabilitation specialist visits. Magnifiers, lighting, safety modifications.
4
Follow-up Calls — Iris checks in regularly. Adjusts support. Connects to community.
5
Independence Maintained — Staying home safely. Connected. Confident. Empowered.
Youth / Identity Journey
1
Meet Them Where They Are — No lectures. No pity. Just real talk.
2
Tech as Equalizer — Phone setup, apps, tools that make school manageable.
3
Peer Connection — Meet other teens who get it. No explanations needed.
4
Sports & Activities — Goalball, cycling, recreation. Belonging through doing.
5
Career Launchpad — Venture Studio. Employment prep. University planning.
Parent / Family Journey
1
Family Support — Iris supports the parent first. Because they need it most.
2
School Advocacy — Accommodation planning. Teacher training. Legal rights.
3
Child Programs — Tech training for kids. Adapted recreation. Peer groups.
4
Parent Network — Connect with families who've been here. You're not alone.
5
Growing Together — As the child grows, Iris adapts. Teen programs. Career prep.

Coming soon

WheelTrans & accessible transit.

Iris books the ride. Direct integration with WheelTrans in Toronto, Access Transit in Vancouver, Para Transpo in Ottawa. "Iris, I need to get to my CNIB tech assessment Tuesday at 2." Done. The single biggest reason members miss appointments is transportation. Iris solves it inside the same conversation.

Coming soon

Be My Eyes handoff.

When a member needs eyes, not a program, Iris hands the moment to Be My Eyes — the AI vision tool 19,000+ blind Canadians already use. "Iris, what's in this can?" One-tap to a sighted volunteer or GPT-4 vision. Iris stays for the navigation; Be My Eyes handles the seeing. The two best tools in the community, working as one.
