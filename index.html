Why Iris Exists

Sight loss arrives without a roadmap.
Iris is the roadmap.

A diagnosis. A doctor's appointment that ends with a pamphlet. A morning in the kitchen when something doesn't look right. And then — nothing. No one tells you what comes next, where to start, or who to call. CNIB has spent a hundred years building the answers. The hard part has always been finding the door. Iris is the door.

The gap Iris fills
Between the moment a person learns they're losing their sight and the moment they find the people who can help, there is a silence. Sometimes it lasts weeks. Sometimes years. Sometimes forever. Iris is what lives in that silence. A guide who picks up on the first try, knows the whole ecosystem, and stays for the next conversation, and the one after that.
How Iris Works

She listens in four layers.
She matches on five inputs.
Then she stays.

Iris isn't a chatbot. It isn't a directory with a voice. It's a structured way of getting to know a person that mirrors what a great human guide would do — without ever asking them to start over, fill out a form, or repeat themselves to the next person in the chain.

The conversation

Four layers, in this order, every time.

  1. 01
    The Story
    "How did you get here?"
    When was the diagnosis. What's changed. What's been the hardest part to put into words. Iris listens for the emotional truth beneath the facts before anything else.
  2. 02
    The Life
    "Who are you beyond the diagnosis?"
    Work, family, what you used to love, what you're afraid of losing. This is where Iris builds a picture of a whole person — not a condition.
  3. 03
    The Need
    "What's hardest right now? Today. This week."
    Not in general — specifically. Practical, social, professional, financial, or emotional. The immediate need is where Iris starts. That's what earns trust.
  4. 04
    The Hope
    "What does a good life look like, on your terms?"
    Not CNIB's terms. Yours. The north star Iris navigates toward — for as long as the relationship lasts.
The match

Five inputs. The one program that's right for you, today.

CNIB has a hundred programs. Most don't fit most people. Iris's job isn't to list them — it's to surface the two or three that fit this person, in this place, at this moment in their life.

Diagnosis
Low vision. Total blindness. Deafblind. Newly diagnosed. Degenerative. Stable.
Geography
Province, city, rural or urban. Many programs are region-locked. Iris only surfaces what's actually available to you.
Life stage
Child. Youth. Working age. Senior. New parent. Newly retired. Each stage opens different doors.
Primary concern
Independence. Employment. Connection. Technology. Education. Family. Money. The thing that's loudest right now.
Interests & lifestyle
Active. Creative. Professional. Caregiver. The texture of a real life — so the match feels like a fit, not a referral.

Two ways in

Reaching members directly.
And through every door they already walk through.

There are two reasons most Canadians with sight loss never find CNIB. They don't know it exists. Or they do, and the moment they could have used it passed without anyone handing them a card. Iris was built for both.

For members
and families.

A senior who has slowly stopped leaving the house. A working adult quietly burning out from compensating. Iris picks up. Asks the right questions. Surfaces the right support. And stays.

A real journey

Margaret · 18 months · one Iris.

This is what continuity actually looks like — not a one-shot Q&A, not a directory lookup. A guide who picks up where she left off, every time.

Day 1
The first call. Margaret's daughter convinces her to dial in. Iris doesn't open with an intake form. She opens with: "What brought you here today?" The conversation lasts 22 minutes. By the end Iris has matched her with a Vision Mate in Sudbury and sent CNIB Beyond Print.
Week 1
The match lands. Linda — also a retired teacher, also living with macular degeneration — visits Margaret's home. Iris texts the next morning: "How was it?" Margaret writes back: "I cried. In a good way."
Month 1
The follow-up Iris promised. Margaret mentions that mornings are still hard. Iris suggests the online peer group — Tuesday at 7pm, three other former teachers in it. Margaret says yes before she has time to talk herself out of it.
Month 3
The doctor's visit. Margaret's ophthalmologist confirms her vision is stable. She tells Iris before she tells her daughter. Iris asks the question no one else thought to: "What does a good year look like for you?"
Month 6
The first event. CNIB Sudbury holds a community lunch. Margaret almost doesn't go. Iris reminds her Linda will be there. Margaret goes. She comes home and tells Iris she signed up for the next one.
Year 1
The shift. Iris notices Margaret asking different questions — less "how do I cope" and more "how do I help." Iris surfaces the Vision Mate volunteer program — this time on the other side of the match.
Year 1.5
The handoff. Margaret meets a newly-diagnosed retired teacher named Joan. She brings her tea. She tells her: "Talk to Iris. She'll know what to do." The journey closes a loop and starts a new one.
One member. One Iris. One thread that stays unbroken — even when life doesn't.
The Member Journey

Every person. A different path.

Iris doesn't follow a script. It maps a unique journey based on diagnosis, life stage, emotional readiness, and goals. Here's what that looks like.

2

3
Practical Tools — Technology assessment. Lending library. Phone accessibility setup.
4
Life Navigation — Employment rights. Driving assessment. Workplace accommodations.
5
Long-term Connection — Community. Sports. Advocacy. From member to champion.

1
Warm Outreach — Iris calls. Warm, unhurried. No pressure. Builds trust.
2
Safety Assessment — Falls, kitchen, reading, isolation. Identifies urgent needs.
3
Home Visit — Rehabilitation specialist visits. Magnifiers, lighting, safety modifications.
4
Follow-up Calls — Iris checks in regularly. Adjusts support. Connects to community.
5
Independence Maintained — Staying home safely. Connected. Confident. Empowered.
