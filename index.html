The match

Five inputs. The one program that's right for you, today.

CNIB has a hundred programs. Most don't fit most people. Iris's job isn't to list them — it's to surface the two or three that fit this person, in this place, at this moment in their life.

Diagnosis
Low vision. Total blindness. Deafblind. Newly diagnosed. Degenerative. Stable.
Geography
Province, city, rural or urban. Many programs are region-locked. Iris only surfaces what's actually available to you.
Life stage
Child. Youth. Working age. Senior. New parent. Newly retired. Each stage opens different doors.
Primary concern
Independence. Employment. Connection. Technology. Education. Family. Money. The thing that's loudest right now.
Interests & lifestyle
Active. Creative. Professional. Caregiver. The texture of a real life — so the match feels like a fit, not a referral.

The Member Journey

Every person. A different path.
One intelligent system.

Iris doesn't follow a script. It maps a unique journey based on diagnosis, life stage, emotional readiness, and goals. Here's what that looks like.

New Diagnosis Journey
1
First Contact — Iris listens. No intake forms. Understands diagnosis, fears, life context.
2
Emotional Support — Peer counseling referral. Vision Mate match. "You're not alone."
3
Practical Tools — Technology assessment. Lending library. Phone accessibility setup.
4
Life Navigation — Employment rights. Driving assessment. Workplace accommodations.
5
Long-term Connection — Community. Sports. Advocacy. From member to champion.
Senior / Phone Journey
1
Warm Outreach — Iris calls. Warm, unhurried. No pressure. Builds trust.
2
Safety Assessment — Falls, kitchen, reading, isolation. Identifies urgent needs.
3
Home Visit — Rehabilitation specialist visits. Magnifiers, lighting, safety modifications.
4
Follow-up Calls — Iris checks in regularly. Adjusts support. Connects to community.
5
Independence Maintained — Staying home safely. Connected. Confident. Empowered.
Youth / Identity Journey
1
Meet Them Where They Are — No lectures. No pity. Just real talk.
2
Tech as Equalizer — Phone setup, apps, tools that make school manageable.
3
Peer Connection — Meet other teens who get it. No explanations needed.
4
Sports & Activities — Goalball, cycling, recreation. Belonging through doing.
5
Career Launchpad — Venture Studio. Employment prep. University planning.
Parent / Family Journey
1
Family Support — Iris supports the parent first. Because they need it most.
2
School Advocacy — Accommodation planning. Teacher training. Legal rights.
3
Child Programs — Tech training for kids. Adapted recreation. Peer groups.
4
Parent Network — Connect with families who've been here. You're not alone.
5
Growing Together — As the child grows, Iris adapts. Teen programs. Career prep.

Iris v1.2.

She listens better. She holds the thread. She hands off like a pro.

Nine upgrades shipped this week — spanning the conversation, the voice layer, and the ground beneath them both. Here's what changed, and why it matters for the people Iris is built for.

The Conversation

She holds the thread.

Iris now remembers the details of your conversation as it unfolds — your name, your city, your diagnosis, the worry you shared ten minutes ago. She weaves them back in when it matters, like a friend who's actually paying attention. No more starting over. No more repeating yourself.

She opens with the truth.

Iris tells you upfront she's a beta — that she can't register you for programs or pick up the phone for you yet. It's a small change that earns trust the moment the conversation starts.

She hands you off like a pro.

When Iris can't take the action herself, she gives you the real CNIB number and writes you an opening line — so your first call lands on exactly the right desk, with the right person, already halfway there.

She notices your silence.

Twenty seconds of quiet and Iris checks in — gently, not pushy. Because sometimes a pause isn't a pause. Sometimes it's the hardest part of the conversation.

The Voice Layer

Voice and text, perfectly in sync.

The words on screen now land exactly when Iris says them. No empty bubble hanging in the air. No lag between what you hear and what you read. Just the rhythm of a real conversation.

She catches your name.

Say just your first name and Iris picks it up — no full sentence required. The small things that make her feel human.

iPhone voice mode, rock-solid.

Safari used to quietly drop the mic mid-conversation. Not anymore. Heartbeat, backoff, and error recovery now keep voice mode alive for as long as you need it — on any phone.

The Ground Beneath

Guardrails that hold.

A second AI pass now reviews every response before it leaves Iris's mouth — catching hallucinated programs, wrong phone numbers, or anything that drifts from what CNIB actually offers. If Iris isn't sure, she says so.

Built to run wide.

New rate limits and structured error logging on the API layer. The pilot can open its doors without surprise costs or silent failures — and every issue now leaves a trace we can learn from.
